title: In-vitro derived germinal centre B cells
date: 2019-09-02 12:35:31
tags:

  1. Question: How do helper T cells activate B cells?

Lakna, (2018, March 26). How Do Helper T Cells Activate B Cells.

  1. Note:

    In the TD response, the CD40-CD40 ligand (CD40L) interaction is required for Th-mediated B-cell activation and thus for GC formation, and for generation of memory B cell. In addition, IL-21signalling is required for IgG1 production, efficient GC formation, affinity maturation and LLPC formation, but not for memory B cell development in mice.

    IL-4 induced BCR class switching from IgM to IgG1 and IgE, generating discrete populations of IgG1+ and IgE+ cells.

  2. Question: How to culture memory T cells in vitro?

    Myoungjoo et al. Bio Protoc. 2017

    1. Note:

      The co-expression of membrane-bound CD40L and BAFF is required for the feeder cells to support optimum proliferation of B cells (culture with IL-4 for 4 days and subsequently culture for 2 more days with IL-21).

  1. Question: How to gate in-vitro-induced GC B (iGB) cells?

    B220+Ig+CD138-GL-7+Fas+CD38lowPNA+.

    And also the upregulation of Bcl-6 and Bcl-xL.

  2. Question: How to test the function of iMB cells?

    (1) Cell cycle test with BrdU (0, 2 and 8 weeks);

    (2) BCR signalling;

    (3) Antibody responses.
